\textbf{Invariants and identities to verify:}
\begin{enumerate}[noitemsep]
  \item \emph{Defining identity}: an equation that should hold at the
        canonical inputs within float tolerance.
  \item \emph{Direct numerical match}: the value of a quantity from the
        paper's table, reproduced from its closed form.
  \item \emph{Global property}: an inequality or invariant that holds
        across a range of inputs (not just the canonical case).
  \item \emph{Closed-form vs.\ numerical agreement}: a derivative or
        integral given in closed form agrees with a numerical equivalent.
  \item \emph{Monotonicity / sign}: a sensitivity that should have a
        definite sign under stated assumptions.
\end{enumerate}
\end{validation}
